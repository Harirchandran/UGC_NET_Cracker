## Examination pattern

Configured from the official **UGC-NET June 2026 Information Bulletin**:

- Computer Based Test
- Paper 1: 50 compulsory MCQs, 100 marks
- Paper 2: 100 compulsory MCQs, 200 marks
- Total: 150 questions, 300 marks
- Duration: 180 minutes without a break
- Two marks per correct response
- No negative marking

Official bulletin:

`https://cdnbbsr.s3waas.gov.in/s301eee509ee2f68dc6014898c309e86bf/uploads/2026/04/202604301078678748.pdf`

## Question provenance

The 150 bundled MCQs are original practice material created specifically for this offline app. They are mapped to the official units but are **not represented as official PYQs**. This prevents uncertain provenance and avoids silently copying commercial question banks.

## Offline previous-year paper library

The app also bundles 17 source-labelled UGC-NET Computer Science and
Applications question-paper PDFs covering every year from 2015 through 2024.
They are intentionally kept separate from the generated practice bank so the
original paper wording, figures, and older examination formats remain intact.
The service worker pre-caches these local files; they can be opened without an
internet connection once the updated app has loaded successfully.

The 2015-2018 legacy files originate from the UGC-NET archive. The 2021 file
is NTA-hosted. Other public paper copies are labelled with their hosting source
inside the app. For scoring any publisher-hosted copy, verify against the
relevant official final answer key.

## Future official changes

An offline package cannot independently know about notices published after it was created. The student should verify a new cycle’s bulletin, dates, pattern and syllabus through the official NTA/UGC pages. The target date and target score remain editable so the app does not hardcode an unannounced future schedule.
